Humpback whale

The humpback whale (Megaptera novaeangliae) is a species of baleen whale.
It is a rorqual; a member of the family Balaenopteridae. Adults range in length from 14–17 m (46–56 ft) and weigh up to 40 metric tons (44 short tons).
The humpback has a distinctive body shape, with long pectoral fins and a knobbly head. It is known for breaching and other distinctive surface behaviors,
making it popular with whale watchers. Males produce a complex song typically lasting 4 to 33 minutes.
